# Clinical Interpretation

This analysis suggests that intradialytic hypotension (IDH) is not primarily driven by session-level variables such as ultrafiltration rate or interdialytic weight gain.

Instead, patient-specific hemodynamic susceptibility appears to play a central role.

## Clinical Implications

- Fixed ultrafiltration thresholds may not be sufficient
- Patient-level risk stratification may improve outcomes
- Hemodynamic phenotyping could guide personalized dialysis prescriptions

## Future Directions

- External validation in multi-center cohorts
- Integration with continuous monitoring data
- Development of predictive ML models
